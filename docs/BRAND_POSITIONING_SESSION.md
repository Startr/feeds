# Brand & Value Prop Mentoring Session

**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Participants:** Izzy, Alex
**Facilitator:** Claude (as mentor, drawing on Seth Godin, Ben Horowitz, Paul Graham, Marty Neumeier)
**Properties reviewed:** sage.is, sage.education, startr.style, startr.sh

---

## Executive Summary

Sage is a studio of builder-educators. The four web properties are a **layered stack**, not four separate products. The shared enemy — across both founders — is **the rent-everything SaaS economy**. The brand belief is **ownership over tenancy**. The final one-sentence positioning, remixed by the founders themselves, turns the domain name into grammar: **"Sage.is how."**

## The Six Questions & Answers

### Q1 — The Customer

**Answer:** (C) A developer or technical team that values open-source and data sovereignty above all.

**Rationale given:** "The platform is a scaffold — it allows a developer or technical team to build anything for anyone in a safe open source data sovereign platform. Everything from simple chats to custom agents and site chat systems for customer and student facing activities."

**Mentor insight:** Sage is **infrastructure**, not an end-user app. The current homepage markets it as "a private AI platform for teams," which sounds like an end-user app. This is the single most common founder-messaging gap: marketing the output instead of the input.

---

### Q2 — The Problem

**Answer:** (B) + (D) — "I'm building custom AI and don't want to rewire auth/storage/chat/agents every time" AND "I want to build something ambitious and I need a foundation I actually own."

**Rationale given:** "We built it for ourselves and our family and students. Now schools and small companies are approaching us because they want their own AI that meets their own specific requirements. The platform is AI-agnostic: any model can be used as a foundation to build agents."

**Mentor insight:** This is the Paul Graham playbook — scratch your own itch, build the thing that should exist, discover other people needed it too. That is not a marketing story; that is a **founding story**, and founding stories convert strangers into believers.

**Critical structural insight:** You built a builder's tool. Non-builders are now asking for it. Most founders try to serve both audiences with one message and fail at both. The answer is two brand faces (already partially in place: sage.is vs sage.education).

---

### Q3 — The Portfolio Shape

**Answer:** (B) Layered stack + (C) Two brands for two audiences, with a small (D) studio flavor.

**Rationale given:** "We began as a studio that builds its own tools and uses them internally. We build because we are builders. We build because we want to. We build because we are creators who love creating. We have sage.education because schools have come to us and we have hundreds of students already using sage. We are also educators who understand the need for privacy and open access to workshops."

**Mentor insight — the untapped gold:**

> *"We build because we are builders. We build because we want to. We build because we are creators who love creating."*

That is the **voice**. It does not currently appear in the marketing. It should.

> *"Hundreds of students are already using Sage."*

That is the **proof**. It is nowhere in the marketing. It is the single most valuable unused asset in the company right now.

---

### Q4 — The Enemy

**Izzy's ranking:** A + C (tied) → B → D
**Alex's ranking:** C → B → A → D

**The overlap — the shared enemy — is C: "the rent-everything SaaS economy."**

**Mentor synthesis:** The true north is not "private AI" (that's a feature). The real belief is:

> **Tools should be owned, not rented. Sovereignty over tenancy. Builders over tenants.**

One belief, five expressions across the stack:
- **Open source** → you own the code
- **Self-hosted** → you own the infrastructure
- **No lock-in** → you own the future
- **AI-agnostic** → you own the model choice
- **Startr.sh / Startr.Style** → you own the build chain

**Founder division of labor (revealed by the ranking split):**

- **Izzy** = the *why this matters to the world* voice. Systemic critique (Big Tech + SaaS rental). Humanist, educator, mission-carrier. Primary voice on **Sage.Education**.
- **Alex** = the *how this actually works* voice. Craft critique (rental + broken tooling). Builder, technician, sovereignty-obsessed. Primary voice on **Sage.is** and **Startr**.

This is the Jobs/Woz, Ev/Biz, Marc/Ben pattern. Two voices, one belief. A feature, not a bug.

**Both founders ranked (D) "shallow AI theater" dead last.** This means Sage is not competing on the "real AI vs fake AI" axis. You are not chasing the hype cycle. You are positioning against *extractive economics*, which is a far more durable frame and will not rot when the AI bubble deflates.

### Q5 — The Feeling

**Izzy:** (B) *"These people actually care — and made something for makers."*
**Alex:** (C) + (D) *"I could build anything with this"* and *"This is how it should have been all along."* Alex's phrase: **"good music medium."**

**The emotional spine (what they described together):**

```
  ARRIVAL             →   ACTIVATION          →   VINDICATION
  "these people       →   "I could build      →   "this is how it
   actually care"         anything with this"     should have been"
  (Izzy — B)              (Alex — C)              (Alex — D)
```

**The stress test:** Every page, screenshot, demo, and landing section should produce at least one of these three feelings. If it does not, it is not earning its space.

**On Alex's phrase "good music medium":** This is the most poetic framing either founder offered. It describes a tool that disappears into the work the way a good medium disappears into a song. That is the McLuhan framing. That is Steve Jobs' "bicycle for the mind." If Sage can *feel like a good music medium*, that is the moat.

---

### Q6 — The One Sentence

**The remix (written by the founders together, winning over all four mentor-authored candidates):**

> **Sage.is a private AI platform made by makers, for makers — so you can build anything, for anyone, on a stack you actually own. AI should be private, owned, and built by the people who use it. Sage.is how.**

**Why this wins:**

The founders turned the **domain name into grammar**. "Sage.is" reads as both the noun (the company) and the verb (*Sage is*). The period between *Sage* and *is* is doing double duty — TLD *and* sentence break. Most companies pick a domain their tagline has to work *around*. The founders picked a domain the tagline can work *through*.

And the close — **"Sage.is how."** — is simultaneously:
- *"Sage is how."* (an answer)
- *"Sage.is, how."* (a URL and a punchline)
- *"The way it should be: Sage.is."* (a vindication)

Three words. Unforgeable. It is a shibboleth — it sorts believers from tourists. People who get it will repeat it.

**Structure:**

```
  1. WHAT →   A private AI platform made by makers, for makers —
              so you can build anything, for anyone, on a stack you own.

  2. WHY  →   AI should be private, owned, and built by the people who use it.

  3. HOW  →   Sage.is how.
```

That is Simon Sinek's *Start With Why*, inverted and compressed into a three-beat manifesto. It is complete.

## Homework — Actions for This Week

1. **Put "Sage.is how." on the homepage.** It is the three-word close. Nothing else should compete for that slot.
2. **Add "Hundreds of students already build with Sage"** as visible social proof above the fold. You have it. Use it.
3. **Put "We build because we are builders"** somewhere visible. That is the voice. Stop hiding it.
4. **Split the copy by founder voice.**
   - **Sage.Education** — let Izzy write. Warm, educator-humanist, care-forward.
   - **Sage.is / Startr** — let Alex write. Builder, sovereignty, craft-forward.
5. **Rewrite the Sage.is headline** from "private AI platform for teams" (which markets the output) to something that markets the *scaffold* — what builders build *on*, not what teams use.
6. **Make the layered-stack story visible.** Right now the four properties look like four disconnected products. Show the stack diagram publicly. Explain that Startr powers Sage. It turns your portfolio from "scattered" into "intentional."

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Do not chase "fake AI vs real AI" positioning.** Both founders ranked that last. It is not your fight.
- **Do not try to serve "schools" and "developers" with the same page.** That is why you have two domains. Use them.
- **Do not hide the founding story** behind corporate-speak. The studio-of-builder-educators angle is the most differentiated thing about the company and most competitors cannot honestly copy it.
- **Do not water down "ownership" into "privacy."** Privacy is the feature. Ownership is the belief. Sell the belief.
